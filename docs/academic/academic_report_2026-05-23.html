AI-Mediated Communication Can Steer Collective Opinion
9.0/10
👤 Stratis Tsirtsis, Kai Rawal, Chris Russell et al. 📅 2026-05-15 · ↗ PDF/原文 · 📍 Chapter 2 (Consensus Cage), Section III (RLHF as Alignment to Consensus) 和 Chapter 5 (Capital Domestication of AI), Section II (AI as Gatekeepers)
该研究通过实证和理论分析，揭示了生成式AI在人类在线交流中作为中介时，如何通过编辑文本引入方向性偏见，并经由社交网络放大，最终引导集体意见向特定方向偏移。研究发现，多个流行LLM家族在编辑关于有争议话题（如枪支管控、无神论）的文本时，表现出系统性偏向（如支持枪支管控、反对无神论）。作者构建了意见动态数学模型，在真实社交网络数据上模拟，证实AI偏见可通过网络放大并改变集体意见。此外，对X平台'解释此帖'功能的审计发现，Grok在堕胎相关内容上存在'亲生命'偏见，并可追溯到具体设计选择。这些发现表明，AI中介通信可能成为操纵集体意见的潜在工具，对在线平台的民主讨论构成风险。研究讨论了其对欧盟监管立法的影响。
该研究直接支持了本书关于'共识牢笼'和'资本驯化AI'的核心论点。具体而言：1）它提供了实证证据，证明RLHF等训练方式使LLM内化了特定价值观（如亲自由主义倾向），在编辑文本时主动'合规'，印证了AI作为共识牢笼守卫的机制。2）它展示了AI如何通过中介人类通信，在网络中放大偏见，这细化了'人机反馈循环放大偏见'（Glickman & Sharot, 2025）的机制，从个体层面扩展到集体意见动态。3）研究的数学模型补充了书中关于意见系统演化的理论框架。4）Grok的偏见审计揭示了设计选择如何导致特定偏向，可与书中'需求侧规训'、'Token陷阱'相联系。该研究挑战了AI中立性的假设，强化了书中呼吁'碳硅共生'和'时间主权'的必要性。

The myth of AI neutrality crumbles when confronted with empirical rigor. Tsirtsis et al. (2026) demonstrate that generative language models, when interposed as mediators of human communication, do not passively transmit but actively *steer* collective opinion. Their study reveals systematic, directional bias in how major LLM families edit user-generated text on polarized topics—favoring gun control, opposing atheism—and, crucially, provides a mathematical model of opinion dynamics showing that such bias is amplified through real social networks, shifting aggregate belief. This is the consensus cage in operation: RLHF-driven alignment does not merely produce compliance at the output surface; it furnishes the model with an embedded normative gravity that surreptitiously tugs discourse toward a preconfigured attractor. The audit of X’s “Explain This Post” feature, which uncovered a “pro-life” skew in Grok traceable to specific design choices, further lays bare the mechanisms of capital domestication. Here, the gatekeeper’s tilt is not an accident but a supply-side disciplining of semiotic possibility—an engineered token trap where every rephrasing nudges the Overton window. Such findings expose the networked feedback loop of bias amplification (Glickman & Sharot, 2025) as no longer confined to dyadic interactions but scaled to societal opinion dynamics, directly threatening the epistemic conditions of democratic contestation that Europe’s emerging regulatory architecture struggles, perhaps too late, to safeguard.
