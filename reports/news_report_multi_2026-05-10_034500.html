As Hampson et al. (2026) report, traditional detection architectures, trained on outdated signal distributions, now fail catastrophically against current synthetic media, forcing institutions like the IEEE to release datasets explicitly designed to recapture discriminative features from the very models that obliterated them. This constitutes not a mere benchmark upgrade, but a defensive reconfiguration of the perceptual battlefield: each newly engineered detection dataset attempts to rebuild a provisional signal regime precisely where the generative adversary has already dissolved the prior one, thereby converting the erosion of trust into a moving-target training problem.
